Has The Dubai Cost Of Living Increased From 2022 To 2023?

Dubai is one of the cities in the world that is considered on priority for migration because of the ample living opportunities one can get here. It provides both the most affordable and the most luxurious life. In past, Dubai witnessed a downtrend in the price because of the decline in education and transportation. The cost of living decreased in 2022 in comparison to 2021 but the pandemic was the biggest reason behind it. It decreased to around 89% for single and 32% for a family of four. But the trend changed in 2023 with the average cost of living increasing from AED 3,435.71 to AED 3,477.89 for a single person and AED 11,863.31 to AED 11,958.62 for a family of four.

FAQs

Q. Is housing in Dubai cheap? 

With the numerous projects and home supply in the market, the Dubai housing market stays relatively cheaper than other markets of the world. Owning a home in Dubai is not a tough task.
 
Q. Is there any tax on income in Dubai?

No, there is no tax on income in UAE means that you can save more. Within the country, there is no individual tax applicable. 
 
Q. How much is the average rent in Dubai?

The UAE average rent lies between 3K - 5K AED for a studio apartment and this increases with the increasing size, changing the property, and location. There are so many factors determining the price and rent. 
 
Q. How much does a tourist spend in Dubai? 

On average, a tourist spends around AED730- the average price of expenses. It includes accommodation, food, traveling, etc.
