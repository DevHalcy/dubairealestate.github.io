Hussain Sajwani- Know All The Important Details About Him In & Out

Early Life - Know The Background

Being the child of entrepreneur parents Hussain Sajwani got the business tricks and leadership in his genes. His father was a trader and had a shop at the local souk. He was from a medical background and attended medical college in Baghdad and obtained a government scholarship. Dropping from the first year, he then left for the US and then pursued industrial engineering at the University of Washington.

Career- How He Started And What He Is Today 

Abu Dhabi Gas Industries was the first organization that he joined and started his career in 1981 in the finance department. Working there for two years, he then started a catering venture, now called Global Logistics Services still in operation. The customers basically included the US military and Bechtel.

Then taking a step forward in 2002, Hussain Sajwani founded DAMAC properties i.e. currently one of the largest property development companies in the Middle East.

In October 2011, DAMAC Properties entered the hospitality division with the name- DAMAC Maison Hotels and Resorts. Then in 2015, the company was publicly listed on the Dubai Financial Market.

The builder has presented renowned property options including a golf course designed by Tiger Woods and property managed by The Trump Organization, luxury apartments and properties like DAMAC Beverly Hills Drive, DAMAC Safa Two, and more designed by the high-end brands like Versace, Fendi, and Roberto Cavalli. Then he came up with DAMAC Tower London in Nine Elms investing £600 million in London, UK. Then after resigning as the chairman of this group, he offered to take the company private.

CSR- Corporate Social Responsibility

This is all about the integration of companies for social and environmental concerns. Hussain Sajwani DAMAC Foundation (HSDF) is deeply rooted in charitable initiatives and assisting the marginalized sectors. Hussain Sajwani DAMAC is determined in providing support to the needies across countries through CSR activities.

  • DAMAC Foundation together with UAE Vice President and Prime Minister sponsored Dubai Future Foundation’s One Million Arab Coders Initiative to provide free software development training to one million young Arabs.
  • As part of the Arab Hope Makers initiative's Humanitarian Cause of the Year for 2020, the foundation committed AED 3 million to the Magdi Yacoub Global Heart Centre in Egypt.
  • In April 2020, the Hussain Sajwani DAMAC Foundation took a step toward the UAE's biggest food distribution drive by pledging Dh 1 million. The meal was for those affected by the Coronavirus.
  • Then HSDF aimed for 1 Billion Meals at the start of Ramadan 2022 with the cost involving AED 5 million. All 50 countries will be provided the food parcels across 50 countries.
  • DAMAC Hussain Sajwani Foundation also contributed to the Dubai Schools Project, allocating AED 20 million to go towards tuition for Emirati youth in need.
  • Then the Al Hadaf Centre for doing Humanitarian causes. This included the help of the Al Hadaf Centre for People of Determination, Dubai Autism Centre, and Al-Tariq Rehabilitation Centre.

Milestones He Achieved

Personal Life- His Family Tree

He was born in the year 1954 into a conservative, middle-class family in Dubai. He received his graduate degree in economics from the University of Washington; the main reason behind the degree was his want of pursuing his career as a white-collar professional.

  • His father has a watch and pen store and handled various real estate projects in Dubai.
  • His mother used to purchase fabric from the wholesale market and hawk goods locally.

Hussain Sajwani was the eldest son of the family and this is the reason why his father wanted him to join the business early.

He is married and has four children; his son Ali Sajwani is an economics graduate from the Northeastern University in Boston, US, and the general manager of operations at DAMAC Group and his daughter Amira Sajwani is a graduate from University College London and postgraduate from the London School of Economics. She is a DAMAC’s Senior Vice President- of Operations.
